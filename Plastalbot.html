PLASTAL-BOT THE LOCOMOTIVE ROBOT

INTRODUCTION

The motivation behind our aspirations of building a robot out of recycled materials was born primarily because of the obsession we both had with robots. Despite not having technical knowledge or experience, we were overly enthusiastic to work on something given the passion we had while attaching matters of climate change and environmental pollution as global predicaments dear to our hearts. Our project’s name is ‘plastalbot’ , a name that has been derived from the word plastic and robot respectively. The idea was to create a robot that performs locomotive functions like human beings do, so that on a few instructions: the robot can perform dexterous functions of course to a limited extent.

We managed to find components from worn-out household appliances such as DVDs and toy cars, which allowed us to work on a few prototypes before we could even commence work on the final project, despite not having our homes close to each other, we manage to converge at a workspace that was conveniently close to our respective homes and we also tried to do the most in those few hours we spent to do our work. We went as far as doing personal research and short courses in basic computer programming, AI, and electronics while acquiring intermediate skills in the field of electronics associated with robotics and artificial intelligence. It was a simple task to access the plastic materials we used, as they were obtained from day-to-day household products like plastic buckets and bottles. I believe the mindset we had too played a role in the success we made, the respect we had for each other, and then working together as a team was the things that helped us achieve what we have achieved so far.

The fact that plastics are inexpensive, durable, and very adaptable for various uses; they become the top most preferred choice for manufacturers and users than any other material. Plastics release toxic chemicals into the environment and they may cause biological damage to various, the chemical structure of most plastics renders them resistant to many natural processes of degradation, and as a result, they are slow to degrade thus allowing a large volume of plastics entering our environments as mismanaged waste and for it to persist in the ecosystem. It is so disheartening to see that majority of people, especially the youths living in our communities are not educated on the hazards of plastic pollution to the environment. THE ANATOMY

BRAINS

  • Arduino UNO Rev3

    • The top choice for our robot’s brain is a microcontroller, and in this case an Arduino UNO microcontroller is used as the center of intelligence for plastalbot. The Arduino is connected to almost every functional part of our robot that requires logic information to operate as instructed, like; the motor drivers and LEDs to mention a few.

ELECTRICAL POWER

  • Power supply

    • Plastalbot’s power source is a 12volts combination of 8 x 1.5V batteries (AAA) connected in a daisy-chain and parallel way to increase the operating time which is its capacity and output voltage to the motor Driver model.

  • On/off Switch

    • A switch is attached on its side to activate and disable plastalbot for maintenance and storage.

  • Breadboard

    • A breadboard is used to connect the electronic components with jumper wire inside the body.

  • Power regulation

    • Fixed Resistors have been used to reduce the flow of current going to the LEDs and to provide protection against excessive flow of current and to adjust the levels of voltage.

MOVEMENT

  • Servo Motor

    • The arms and head’s movement is made by servo Motors connected from the inside of the body.

  • D.C Motor

    • Even though home-made robots move on a pair of wheels like most automobiles, plastalbot has been built with a pair of legs that are capable of mimicking human being’s upright kind of walking with the support of D.C gear motors, motors place distinctive way that produces alternative movements.

  • L293N MotorDriver

    • Normally, D.C motors have two leads, of which one is positive and the other is negative thus if connected to a battery, the motor will rotate in one direction and when the leads are exchanged the motor will rotate in the opposite direction. It is nearly impossible to create a robot that makes rhythmic movements with D.C motors that are connected directly to a power source thus to have total control over the direction of the D.C gear motor without changing the leads, a L293N Motor Driver circuit has been used to control plastalbot’s movement.
